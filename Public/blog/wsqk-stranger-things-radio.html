Fan-Made Stranger Things Radio: From WSQK The Squawk to WFLK The Flock

How fans created a real 80s radio experience inspired by the fictional Hawkins, Indiana station

When Netflix's Stranger Things introduced the world to WSQK "The Squawk", it wasn't just another background detail in the show's meticulously crafted 1980s aesthetic. It became a symbol of everything we love about vintage radio – and inspired fans to bring that magic into the real world.

What Made WSQK The Squawk Special

WSQK The Squawk is the fictional radio station from Hawkins, Indiana that appears throughout Stranger Things. Broadcasting the hits of 1983-1986, The Squawk represents the golden age of small-town American radio: charismatic DJs, local commercials, community announcements, and a curated soundtrack that perfectly captures the era.

For many viewers, The Squawk wasn't just part of the show – it felt like something they wanted to actually tune into. That desire sparked a movement.

The Birth of WFLK The Flock

Enter WFLK The Flock – a fan-made radio station that brings the spirit of WSQK to life. While we can't use Netflix's intellectual property directly, we've created something even better: a fully realized 80s radio experience that broadcasts 24/7.

Think of us as the station from the next town over. While The Squawk was serving Hawkins, The Flock was keeping neighboring communities entertained with the same incredible music, the same vintage vibes, and the same community spirit that made 80s radio legendary.

What You'll Experience

  • Non-stop 80s hits – From synthpop to rock anthems, hair metal to new wave
  • Authentic DJ breaks – Period-accurate hosting style that transports you back in time
  • Vintage commercials – Fictional ads that capture small-town charm
  • Easter eggs – Hidden references for Stranger Things fans
  • Active community – Join thousands of fans on Discord
"The signal never dies. The Flock flies on." – Our motto captures everything we stand for: keeping the spirit of 80s radio alive for generations to come.
